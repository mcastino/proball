4. Code of Conduct

4.1 Players

All participants are expected to:

  • Treat coaches, staff, fellow participants, and spectators with respect at all times.
  • Follow coach instructions and programme rules.
  • Behave in a manner consistent with the values of fair play, effort, and teamwork.
  • Not engage in bullying, intimidation, harassment, or discriminatory behaviour of any kind.
  • Not use abusive, offensive, or threatening language on or off the court at any ProBall venue.

4.2 Parents & Guardians

Parents and guardians are expected to:

  • Encourage positive participation and refrain from placing undue pressure on their child or others.
  • Respect coaching decisions and not interfere with sessions while in progress.
  • Model the behaviour they wish to see in young athletes.
  • Not approach opposing players, coaches, or officials in a confrontational manner at any competition.

4.3 Breach of Code

ProBall reserves the right to issue warnings, suspend, or permanently exclude any participant or parent/guardian who breaches this Code of Conduct. No refund will be issued in the event of exclusion for conduct-related reasons.

5. Health & Safety

5.1 Medical Conditions

You must disclose any medical conditions, injuries, allergies, or disabilities that may affect the participant's ability to safely take part in basketball activities at the time of registration. ProBall coaches are not medical professionals and will act in good faith based on the information provided.

5.2 Injury

Basketball is a physical sport and carries an inherent risk of injury. By registering, you acknowledge and accept this risk. ProBall coaches hold current First Aid certifications and will render appropriate first aid in the event of injury. Serious injuries will result in the emergency services being called.

5.3 Unwell Participants

Participants who are unwell, infectious, or showing symptoms of illness should not attend sessions. If a participant becomes unwell during a session, a parent or guardian will be contacted to collect the child.

5.4 Working with Children

All ProBall coaches and staff who work directly with participants under 18 hold a current NSW Working with Children Check (WWCC). Copies are available on request.
